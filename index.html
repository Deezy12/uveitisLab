ARVO 2026 · Presentation #4095–0309

GLP-1 Receptor Agonists and Risk of Dry Eye Disease

Does GLP-1 receptor agonist exposure reduce the incidence of dry eye disease, and is this effect modified by diabetes status?
Central finding: GLP-1RA use was associated with a 16% reduction in dry eye disease risk overall and a 32% reduction in non-diabetic patients, with the strongest protective signal in those without diabetes.
Eliot N. Haddad, BS · Radhika S. Amin, BA, MPH · Sumit Sharma, MD · Rony R. Sayegh, MD Cole Eye Institute, Cleveland Clinic · Case Western Reserve University Contact: sayeghr@ccf.org

Proposed Mechanisms
🔬
Anti-Inflammatory
GLP-1RAs reduce IL-6, IL-1β, and TNF-α — key cytokines implicated in ocular surface inflammation and DED pathogenesis.
💧
Lacrimal Gland
GLP-1 receptors are expressed in lacrimal gland and ocular adnexal tissues. Topical liraglutide improved tear secretion in diabetic murine models; human studies report higher Schirmer values and longer TBUT.
⚖️
Metabolic & Lifestyle
GLP-1RA-associated weight loss and lifestyle modification may reduce DED severity. The stronger signal in non-diabetic patients — where hyperglycemia is absent — supports this as an important driver.
Limitations
  • ICD-10 code-based DED classification may introduce misclassification bias, as dry eye diagnoses are clinically heterogeneous and often assigned by exclusion.
  • Despite propensity matching, BMI and HbA1c remained imbalanced (SMD >0.10) across cohorts, indicating potential residual confounding by metabolic severity.
  • Confounding by indication is plausible — GLP-1RAs, insulin, metformin, and SGLT2i are prescribed to distinct patient profiles with different baseline risk.
  • Unmeasured confounders (disease duration, adherence, inflammatory biomarkers, socioeconomic status, concurrent immunomodulatory therapy) were not captured.
  • No clinical exam metrics were available (TBUT, tear osmolarity, meibography, corneal esthesiometry), limiting clinical granularity.
  • Retrospective design precludes causal inference. An ophthalmology encounter as the index event may introduce immortal time bias.
